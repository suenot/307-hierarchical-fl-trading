# Hierarchical FL: The "Army and General Staff" Analogy

Imagine an army of 1,000 soldiers trying to learn a new combat tactic.

### 1. How it was before (Flat FL)
Every one of the 1,000 soldiers must personally report their progress to the General in the capital every hour. The communication lines are overloaded, the General is buried in minor reports, and soldiers spend more time on calls than training.

### 2. How it works now (Hierarchical FL)
1. **Squad (Edge)**: Soldiers train in groups of 10.
2. **Platoon (Regional Hub)**: A Sergeant collects reports from their 10 soldiers and forms a single summarized conclusion.
3. **Staff (Global Server)**: The General only receives reports from the Sergeants (there are only 100 of them, not 1,000).

### Why use this in trading?
Funds in New York and Tokyo operate in different time zones and on different exchanges. HFL allows the New York offices to quickly agree on local trends (Regional Sync) and then send the "essence" of their best strategies to the Global Server once a day to share knowledge with the Tokyo offices.

**The Result**: The model learns faster while using 80% less network bandwidth.
